## Line Inputs

The DX816/822 consoles feature various types of inputs, which are combined in the console's different channels:

- **2 BAL-LINE**: Balanced inputs.
- **4 AUX LINE**: Unbalanced inputs.
  - **2 RCA** on LINE channels, switchable from the front panel.
  - **2 miniplug** on USB channels, with **automatic switching**: when inserting a TRS plug, the USB audio is muted.

<Info>
  The unbalanced line inputs have a nominal level of **–10 dBV** into **10 KΩ** .

  The gain adjustment is common for both (main/auxiliary). It may require readjustment depending on the connected equipment.
</Info>

### Balanced Input Connections

The balanced inputs use shielded **RJ45** connectors and require shielded Category 5 multi-pair cable. The **MNGDX822** pre-made cable kit is available, which includes all necessary connection cables.

## Microphone Inputs

The microphone inputs are electronically balanced and use standard **XLR connectors**.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Microphones on DX 816">
    <Frame>
      ![DX 816 Rear Panel - Mics](/images/816-trasero-mics-1.jpg)
    </Frame>

    - 5 XLR inputs, labeled MIC 1 through MIC 5
    - MIC 4 shares preamp with MIC 2
    - MIC 5 shares preamp with MIC 3

    <Info>
      **CHANNELS**

      Channel 1 ⟶ MIC-1 Inputs\
      Channel 2 ⟶ MIC-2 and MIC-4 Inputs\
      Channel 3 ⟶ MIC-3 and MIC-5 Inputs
    </Info>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Microphones on DX 822">
    <Frame>
      ![DX 816 Rear Panel - Mics](/images/822_MICS.png)
    </Frame>

    - 10 XLR inputs, labeled MIC 1, 2, 3, 2b, 3b, 4, 5, 6, 5b and 6b
    - MIC-2b shares preamp with MIC-2
    - MIC-3b shares preamp with MIC-3
    - MIC-5b shares preamp with MIC-5
    - MIC-6b shares preamp with MIC-6

    <Info>
      **CHANNELS**

      Channel 1 ⟶ MIC-1 Inputs\
      Channel 2 ⟶ MIC-2 and MIC-2b Inputs\
      Channel 3 ⟶ MIC-3 and MIC-3b Inputs\
      Channel 4 ⟶ MIC-4 Inputs\
      Channel 5 ⟶ MIC-5 and MIC-5b Inputs\
      Channel 6 ⟶ MIC-6 and MIC-6b Inputs
    </Info>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  When using two microphones on the same channel, they must be of the **same make and model** to ensure signal uniformity.
</Warning>

### 48V Phantom Power

The **48V** button on the front panel activates floating phantom power for **all microphones**. 

In the **DX 822** model, there is a second switch that toggles 48V power for the second group of five inputs.

<Info>
  Dynamic microphones tolerate 48V phantom power, allowing safe mixing of dynamic and condenser microphones without risk.
</Info>
