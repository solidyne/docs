## Files for time and weather reports

Below are the different options for generating weather report voiceovers.\
The voiceover can be recorded in various ways, depending on how the audio files are defined in the **Audicom Configuration Panel**, **HTH** section.\
Files are stored in the `Audicom\Meteor` folder.\
Supported formats: **WAV** (recommended) or **MP3**.

**Temperature**, **humidity** and **rainfall** share the numerical files, but **time** does not.

---

## Time announcement

The announcement is generated by combining audio files:

- Example: `"twenty-two hours"` + `"forty-seven minutes"`\
  Files: `H-22.wav` + `M-47.wav`
- For exact hours: `H-nn.wav` + `U-OCLOCK.wav`\
  (the file may contain the phrase **"o'clock"** or **"zero minutes"**).
- Optional files:
  - Initial presentation: `P-HOUR.wav`
  - Closing: `S-HOUR.wav`\
    (phrases like "Radio X presents the time..." or sound effects).

### Variants

- **Play time with seconds**\
  Added:
  - Number of seconds (`N-nnnn.wav`)
  - Unit (`U-SEC.wav` or `U-SECS.wav`)
- **"And" interjection**\
  Insert file `U-Y.wav` between hours and minutes\
  Example: "Fifteen hours **and** twenty-five minutes".
- **24 Hr / 12 Hr Format**
  - `24 Hr Format` (00-23, default).
  - `12 Hr Format` (01-12) with **AM/PM** option.\
    Files: `U-AM.wav`, `U-PM.wav`.

## Temperature announcement

1. **Initial presentation**: `P-TEMP.wav`\
   Example: "The current temperature..."
2. Methods:
   - **Single file**
     - Positive: `T+nnn.wav` (e.g.: `T+024.wav` = "twenty-four degrees").
     - Negative: `T-nnn.wav` (e.g.: `T-003.wav` = "minus three degrees").
   - **Separating "degrees"**
     - Numeric file: `N-nnnn.wav`
     - Unit: `U-DEGS.wav`, `U-DEG.wav`, `U-DEGB0.wav`, `U-DEGSB0.wav`.
   - **Report decimals**
     - `U-DEC.wav` + decimal number (`N-nnnn.wav`)\
       Example:\
       `P-TEMP.wav` → `T+024.wav` → `U-DEC.wav` → `N-0003.wav`\
       → "The current temperature... twenty-four degrees, point three".
3. **Closing**: `S-TEMP.wav`

## Humidity announcement

- **Opening**: `P-HUM.wav` (optional).
- Options:
  - Single file: `A-nn.wav` (e.g.: `A-043.wav` = "43 percent").
  - Separated: `N-nnnn.wav` + `U-PERC.wav`.
- **Closing**: `S-HUM.wav` (optional).

---

## Atmospheric pressure announcement

- **Opening**: `P-PRES.wav` (optional).
- Options:
  - Single file: `N-nnnn.wav` (e.g.: "one thousand ten hectopascals").
  - Separated: `N-nnnn.wav` + `U-HP.wav` or `U-MB.wav`.
- **Closing**: `S-PRES.wav`.

<Note>
  Numbers 950 to 1040 are reserved exclusively for pressure.\
  If using single files, they must include the unit in the voiceover.
</Note>

---

## Rainfall announcement

- **Opening**: `P-RAIN.wav` (optional).
- Options:
  - Single file: `R-nnn.wav` (e.g.: `R-052.wav` = "52 millimeters").
  - Separated: `N-nnnn.wav` + `U-MM.wav` or `U-MMS.wav`.
- **Closing**: `S-RAIN.wav`.

---

## Voice recording

- The announcer must maintain a **natural intonation**.
- It's recommended to record complete phrases and then **edit** to cut into parts.
- Files are saved in `Audicom\Meteor`.
- Can be recorded in **WAV** (preferred) or **MP3**.

### Required files list

Depending on the configured method, some or all may be needed:

- `H-00.wav` … `H-23.wav` → hours.
- `M-01.wav` … `M-59.wav` → minutes.
- `U-OCLOCK.wav` → "o'clock" / "zero minutes".
- `N-0000.wav` … `N-0300.wav` → numbers for temperature, humidity, rainfall.
- `N-0750.wav` … `N-1040.wav` → numbers for pressure.
- `P-HOUR.wav`, `S-HOUR.wav` → time opening and closing.
- `P-TEMP.wav`, `S-TEMP.wav` → temperature opening and closing.
- `P-HUM.wav`, `S-HUM.wav` → humidity opening and closing.
- `P-PRES.wav`, `S-PRES.wav` → pressure opening and closing.
- `P-RAIN.wav`, `S-RAIN.wav` → rainfall opening and closing.
- `T+nnn.wav`, `T-nnn.wav` → positive/negative temperature.
- `A-nn.wav` → humidity (single file mode).
- `R-nnn.wav` → rainfall (single file mode).
- Units:
  - `U-AM.wav`, `U-PM.wav`
  - `U-DEC.wav`
  - `U-DEG.wav`, `U-DEGS.wav`, `U-DEGB0.wav`, `U-DEGSB0.wav`
  - `U-HOUR.wav`, `U-HOURS.wav`
  - `U-HP.wav`, `U-MB.wav`
  - `U-MIN.wav`, `U-MINS.wav`
  - `U-MM.wav`, `U-MMS.wav`
  - `U-PERC.wav`
  - `U-SEC.wav`, `U-SECS.wav`
  - `U-Y.wav`
