## Power Supply

The unit features an internal switching power supply, **110/240V 50/60Hz.**

The rear panel houses the **power switch**, the main fuse (1A), and the Interlock-type power cable connector (supplied with the console).

## Grounding

UX24 connects to ground through the power cable. Do not use adapters or cables that bypass the ground pin. It is also recommended to connect the chassis ground terminal to the facility's ground using 4 mm cable or larger.

The general electrical installation must have proper grounding. For reference, you can check the [FM grounding diagram](/es/puesta-a-tierra).

<Danger>
  **WARNING!**

  **Ensure that the computer connected to UX24 via USB has proper grounding.**
</Danger>

## Inputs, outputs and ports

The rear panel concentrates all console connections. RJ45 connectors are used for all audio connections, except for the headphone and control room monitor outputs.

You will also find **Ethernet** ports, **USB** port, **Tally Light** connections and **GPIO** control.

<Note>
  See [Inputs and Outputs Infographic](http://www.solidyne.dx.com.ar/wordpress-files/DW/doc-guides/UX24_Quickguide_Inputs_and_Outputs_features.pdf) (PDF).
</Note>

<Icon icon="arrow-right-to-line" iconType="solid" color="#008dff" size={22} />  [INPUTS](/es/unidex/instalacion-entradas) (click for more info)

- 4 **microphone** inputs.
  - Use RJ45 connectors. Connected using a **SOL-M25/1XLRF** cable (RJ45 male to XLR female, 2.5 meters). Shorter lengths can also be purchased for XLR connections near the console.

  <Info>
    The RJ45 female **MICROPHONE** inputs use the **LEFT channel** of the balanced connection.
  </Info>
- **16 UNIDEX** mono inputs groupable in stereo pairs.
- **4 balanced** stereo.
- **4 digital** AES3
- **4 USB** stereo audio.
- **1 audio** streaming (replaces AES3-1)
- OPTION: **DANTE** x16

<Note>
  **MODELS WITH DANTE OPTION** (UX24-A67)

  When UX24 includes the DANTE/AES67 option it replaces the inputs:

  **UNIDEX 5, 6, 7 and 8**   Are replaced by AoIP 1, 2, 3 and 4.\
  **BAL 9, 10, 11 and 12**   Are replaced by AoIP 5, 6, 7 and 8.
</Note>
